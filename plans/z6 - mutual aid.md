Some quick notes for now on mutual aid relationships
between the plans:

*   Conceptual model: random needs + TFT-like strategies
    *   Imagine that for two clans with a mutual aid
        relationship, a sequence of random events happens
        where one or the other needs some help
    *   At first, we can imagine that things generally
        happen to balance out over a turn, but later add
        the possibility they don't

*   Trust
    *   Trust is a major factor in how effective mutual
        aid relationships are
    *   The idea is that one clan may fail to help another
        for various reasons: didn't have capacity at the
        time, mistake, recent conflict, etc. The other clan
        will probably retaliate, leading to less cooperation,
        but if the relationship is mutually beneficial,
        they'll probably be able to patch it up. We roll
        all those factors into trust and scale relationship
        benefits by trust
    *   The trust model will have a "target" or "max" trust
        based on certain factors
        *   Trust will slowly decay upward
        *   Trust will rapidly decay downward
    *   Factors
        *   Relatedness/similarity
        *   Alignment
        *   Conflicts
        *   Ability of counterparty to help when it's their turn
        *   Too many relationships make it more likely a clan
            will be called on by two and can't perform for both
        *   Too many relationships make favor debt tracking more
            likely to get out of sync

*   Mutual aid benefits
    *   This is about benefits specifically to mutual help
        relationships - there are other benefits to relationships
        generally
    *   Benefit types
        *   Food security
        *   Stress
        *   Productivity
        *   (Influence/Conflict)
        *   (Alignment)
    *   Amount of aid from any one partner scales with:
        *   Trust
    *   Benefit amount comes as a function of "aid received" with
        diminishing returns

*   Asymmetric relationships
    *   Clans should generally be giving about as much aid as
        they're giving
    *   If scale makes debt tracking hard, we could allow for some
        error
    *   For small favors, we can simply equalize the exchange,
        reducing one side's amount if needed, or allowing the
        other side to pay with favors (small amounts of stress/
        food/labor)
    *   The important one is food security, where clans may suffer
        big losses. There can definitely be a chance for one clan
        to need a lot more than the other.
        *   Exactly what happens in this case is pretty murky, but
            for now, let's assume that if some clan can no longer
            support itself, the other clans will collectively help
            them, but they'll lose a lot of standing
        *   There can also be an option to join another clan or
            move away
        *   Later, we can let individual clans or subsets with
            resources to spare "outbid" the collective for providing
            help
