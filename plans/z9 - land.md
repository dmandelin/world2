Notes on land

The question is how people can come to "own" land and exactly
what that means. Ownership is presumably always in context of
some set of norms (going back to mammalian territorial
instincts). We might assume our early farmers had fairly fluid
practices, but we'll also assume that they at least had to get
the people around them to agree "these crops are mine" for it
to have much of any point.

"We were here first" is probably one of the most important norms.
There might be others, such as "this family is in need right now",
but that probably has much less influence.

At the beginning, we have our farmers working naturally occurring
alluvium. It should be relatively small in quantity, but high in
productivity (including the fact that little prep work is needed).
In this case, it's not entirely clear they would or could keep to
the same fields year after year, but rather might grab what's
available in a given year, which might shift anyway.

Land allocation gets interesting once there isn't enough for
everyone. Presumably there wasn't really a system for this, and
people end up arguing about it. This could be a source of conflict,
but people also might mitigate with turn-taking rules or setting
up an authority.

Soon, we should allow them to use small-scale canal irrigation
to create new farmland. Then, there will start to be variable
quality land, perhaps 3-5 tiers for simplicity. Alternatively,
we could have a quality level on every plot, and then could fold
in clan land improvements. For these, we'll assume that norms
are such that if you live there and build a farm, it's yours...
though that could change as land becomes scarce.
